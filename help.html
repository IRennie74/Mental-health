How to succeed in high school with ADHD

People in high school with ADHD need to: make better study skills, time management tools, and organization strategies. You can make better study skills by going to the teacher if you need help and ask him or her to go over the lessons slower next time you do one. For organization strategies you can clean your desk or locker every week and just bring home things you don't need because it takes up more space.

How ADHD affect high school students

ADHD is a mental health disorder. Some High school students have ADHD (15.4% of students have ADHD in high school). ADHD can do things like make you not want to sit still for long periods of time especially in a calm and quiet place and Not being able to focus on just one task and when both of these happen it is hard to get work done. People with ADHD there brain works differently than other peoples would in a way that they can see things that other people would not even think exist.
